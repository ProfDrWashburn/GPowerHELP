Set your study parameters

These are already set to the most commonly accepted values in nursing research. Adjust only if your proposal requires it.

Significance Level (Alpha)

Your tolerance for a false alarm (Type I error). A 0.05 level means you accept a 5% chance of saying something works when it does not.

Desired Power

The probability your study will detect a real effect if one truly exists. Think of it as your pulse oximeter's sensitivity setting. Higher power means fewer missed findings but requires more participants.

Tip: For most EBP proposals, 80% power is acceptable and expected. Use 90% when missing a real effect would carry significant clinical consequences.
